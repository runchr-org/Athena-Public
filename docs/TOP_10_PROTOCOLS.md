# Top 10 Protocols — Re-Ranked (June 2026)

> **Date**: 17 June 2026
> **Methodology**: Heatmap-weighted MCDA + behavioral evidence + cross-domain pattern validation
> **Sessions Analyzed**: 1,800+ (vs ~400 at original ranking)
> **Key Shift**: Original ranking optimized for *theoretical impact*. This one optimizes for **empirically demonstrated behavioral change**.

These are the 10 most impactful protocols from the Athena framework, re-ranked based on actual usage data and observed behavioral outcomes across 1,800+ sessions.

---

## What Changed Since the Original Ranking

The original TOP_10_PROTOCOLS.md was scored on four criteria: Ruin Prevention (35%), Applicability (30%), Portability (20%), Depth (15%). That was a solid framework — but it was built on *theoretical importance*, not *lived usage data*.

Since then, three things happened:

1. **The Protocol Heatmap matured** — 411 sessions analyzed, showing actual invocation frequency across the entire protocol library
2. **Behavioral tracking** revealed which protocols actually changed behavior in practice (pricing, trading, psychology, fitness)
3. **Cross-domain meta-patterns** crystallized 17 universal laws — some protocols gained importance as carriers of patterns that apply across 5+ domains

The biggest surprise: **several protocols ranked highly on theory are rarely invoked in practice**, while others initially undervalued are the quiet workhorses that run constantly in the background.

## The 5 Promotions (Why They Entered)

### PAT-574: Substance Decode Lens → Rank #2

**What it does**: Separates *form* (what something looks like, what's claimed) from *substance* (what's actually happening, what the data shows). Forces you to test whether the visible narrative matches the underlying reality.

**Old status**: Unranked (existed as a pattern-detection protocol, not a "top 10 candidate")

**Why it rose**: This protocol underpins the **Form-Substance Gap** — the single most referenced meta-pattern across the entire case study corpus. Every domain — food & beverage, relationships, career, trading, fitness, investing — has a Form-Substance failure as the root cause.

**Behavioral evidence**: The fitness domain provides the clearest case. A user can have a world-class fitness documentation system (Form) while body composition deteriorates (Substance). The objective measurement was the Substance Decode moment — and it triggered immediate architectural correction. Pricing calibration shows another: "I work fast, so it must not be worth much" is a Form-Substance error on self-valuation.

**Why higher than Ergodicity**: Ergodicity is critical but narrow — it applies to repeated gambles. Form-Substance applies to *everything*. You check ergodicity before a trade. You need Substance Decode before every decision, relationship, career move, and self-assessment.

---

### P003: Revealed Preference (Actions > Words) → Rank #3

**What it does**: Forces you to judge situations, people, and yourself by *demonstrated behavior* rather than stated intentions. "What do the actions say?" overrides "What do the words say?"

**Old status**: Unranked (considered "obvious")

**Why it rose**: 135 references in the heatmap — the *third most referenced protocol in the entire system*. That's not an accident. Relationship case studies, business case studies, and pricing calibrations all reduce to "what they DO vs what they SAY."

**Behavioral evidence**: The shift from person-level blame to systems-level schema engineering was powered by this principle. "Match YOUR investment to THEIR demonstrated commitment" is Revealed Preference as an operating system. Pricing calibration also encoded it: "Effort = Worth" was a schema error because the *market's* revealed preference was willingness-to-pay, not hours-worked.

**Why it was originally missed**: It seemed too simple. "Actions > Words" feels like common sense, not a protocol. But 135 references across 411 sessions says it's the most *operationally used* principle after Law of Ruin and Ergodicity. Common sense that needs to be repeated 135 times is not common at all.

---

### MP-15 carrier: Preparation Asymmetry → Rank #5

**What it does**: Encodes the universal pattern that front-loading decisions before execution — doing the thinking *before* the doing — produces disproportionate returns. This is the meta-pattern that unifies battleplanning, spec-driven development, pre-trade risk checks, and research protocols.

**Old status**: Not a standalone protocol — it's a meta-pattern

**Why it rose**: The Preparation Asymmetry pattern maps across poker, travel, software engineering, fitness, trading, academic delivery, and service pricing. Every domain where measurable improvement was demonstrated traces back to front-loading decisions before execution.

**Behavioral evidence**: Project execution discipline shows the cleanest evolution: from early projects with no pre-work (low effective hourly rates) to later projects using structured battleplanning (an order-of-magnitude improvement in effective rate). The *same operator* doing the *same type of work* achieved dramatically better outcomes by adding pre-work. This is the strongest empirical signal in the entire dataset.

**Encoding**: This isn't a single protocol file — it's carried by `/battleplan`, `spec-driven-dev`, `trading-risk-gate`, `zenith-execution`, `/brief`, and `red-team-review`. The meta-pattern is the *reason* those skills work.

---

### BUS-96: Income Hierarchy → Rank #8

**What it does**: Classifies every income stream on a hierarchy from Level 0 (time-for-money with a single employer) to Level 3 (asset-based income that compounds). Uses the "Hand-Stop-Mouth-Stop Test" — if your hands stop working, does your income stop? — as the simplest diagnostic.

**Old status**: Unranked (classified as a business protocol, not a "core" protocol)

**Why it rose**: It's the structural encoding of the **Compounding vs Linear** meta-pattern, which explains career trajectory evolution from linear service delivery to building compounding assets. The "Hand-Stop-Mouth-Stop Test" is the simplest diagnostic in the entire system, and it immediately classifies any income stream.

**Behavioral evidence**: The identity evolution from freelance service delivery to building asset-based systems was structurally enabled by recognizing the difference between linear income (trading hours for money) and compounding assets (building systems that generate value independently).

**Cross-domain**: The hierarchy explains food service businesses (pure labor = Level 1), consulting (1:1 delivery vs async), content (one article for one reader vs infinity readers), and knowledge work (accumulated sessions = compounding asset).

---

### DEC-330: Aoy's Fried Rice Principle → Rank #10

**What it does**: "Mastery of fundamentals + execution discipline > complexity." Named after the observation that a street vendor's perfect fried rice — simple ingredients, flawless wok technique — outperforms elaborate dishes. It's the anti-over-engineering protocol.

**Old status**: Unranked

**Why it rose**: This principle targets the most common recurring failure mode: over-engineering. Elaborate documentation systems that don't produce results, over-analyzed trading architectures, complex boot sequences when simple ones suffice — all are violations of the Fried Rice Principle.

**Behavioral evidence**: The architectural evolution toward minimalism — reducing complexity, just-in-time loading, tiered information architecture — all are "wok hei over soy sauce" engineering decisions. Simplicity with mastery beats complexity with mediocrity.

**Cross-domain**: Applies to coding (ship at 80%, iterate), trading (simple systems beat complex ones), fitness (compound lifts > complex routines), pricing (scope gates > elaborate proposals), and content (emotional hooks > structural analysis).

## What This Ranking Reveals About Evolution

The shift from the original ranking to this one tells a story:

```
Original (Jun 6):  Safety → Decision → Reasoning → Engineering
Current  (Jun 17): Safety → Pattern Detection → Behavioral → Meta-Pattern → Decision
```

**Translation**: The early ranking was *intellectually focused* — the best protocols were reasoning tools (Synthetic Parallel, First Principles, Claim Atomization). The current ranking is *behaviorally focused* — the best protocols are the ones that actually changed *actions*, not just *thinking*.

The biggest single insight: **P003 (Revealed Preference) being the 3rd most referenced protocol in 1,800+ sessions means the most common failure mode isn't bad reasoning — it's ignoring what actions reveal.** The system doesn't need better thinking tools. It needs better "seeing what's actually happening" tools.
